The Help Page for the Scav Page

Viewing and commenting on items

To view an item, click on it. To go back to the listing of items, either click/tap the x at the top right, click/tap outside the item display, or use the back button on your browser.

To comment on an item, scroll to the bottom of the item's comment thread and enter your comment. If you wish to format your comment, you can use the standard HTML formatting tools as well as Javascript. Please don't go too overboard with this, or your comment will be subject to deletion.

If you are not logged in, a warning message may appear when you try to comment. If this is the case, just hit the comment button again and your comment will be submitted anonymously.

Registration

You may register an account to identify yourself on the site. No critical functions require registration, though it is encouraged. To reach the registration page, either [click here]{http://macscav.com/registration/} or go through the login button at the top left of the main page. All fields in the registration are required, and you must tick the checkbox at the bottom of the page to verify your humanity.

Adding items

You can reach the item adding page [here]{http://macscav.com/add_item/} or through the footer on the main page. Fill out all the relevant fields for an item, and you will be prompted if you failed to fill out a required field.

Searching

You may search by page, category, or keyword. To search by page (e.g. page N), enter #page:N into the search bar. To search for items across multiple pages (e.g. pages N and M), enter #page:N #page:M into the search bar

To search by category, use #category:category name. If you enter multiple categories, items matching any of these categories will be returned. The following categories are currently implemented:

  • road-trip
  • showcase
  • food
  • music
  • video
  • science
  • arts

To search by keyword, simply type the keyword into the search bar. If you enter multiple keywords separated by spaces, items matching any of the keywords will be returned. This means that if you search for a flaming tiger, your search results will contain all items containing the letter a.

Finally, these three types of queries carry an AND relation between each other. In other words, if you search for #page:4 #page:5 #category:food #category:science hydrogen, the search results will be all items from either page 4 or page 5, which have either food or science as a category, and contain the word hydrogen.
